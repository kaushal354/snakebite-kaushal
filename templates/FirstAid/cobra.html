Indian Cobra First Aid

If you or someone around you has been bitten by a cobra, it is important to seek medical attention immediately. However, there are some first aid measures that can be taken to help alleviate some of the symptoms and improve the
chances of recovery:

  1. Stay calm and still: Do not move around too much or try to run as this will increase blood flow and the spread of the venom.

  2. Remove tight clothing or jewelry: This will allow for proper blood flow and prevent constriction in the affected area.

  3. Wash the wound with soap and water: Gently clean the bite area with soap and water to remove any dirt or debris.

  4. Immobilize the affected limb: If the bite is on an arm or leg, immobilize the limb to prevent the venom from spreading. Use a splint or sling if available.

  5. Apply a cold compress: Use a cold compress, ice pack or cool, wet cloth to reduce swelling and pain.

  6. Drink plenty of water: Staying hydrated will help your body flush out the venom more efficiently.

  7. Seek medical attention: Call emergency services or transport the person to the nearest hospital or clinic for further treatment.

It is important to note that these first aid measures do not replace medical treatment and should be seen as temporary measures until proper medical help can be obtained.
